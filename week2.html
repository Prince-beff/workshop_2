About Me

I'm the kind of person who walks into a room and quietly notices the little things others miss, like a plant that needs water or a joke waiting to be made. I balance curiosity with a laid-back vibe, always ready to try something new but never rushing into anything without a moment of thought. People find it easy to talk to me because I listen with genuine interest, and even though I don’t always show it. Whether exploring a new hobby, getting lost in music, or just chilling with my own thoughts, I carry a mix of calm confidence and quiet determination that makes my presence feel steady and real. I appreciate the kind of moments that most people overlook the sound of soft rain tapping on a window, the warmth of sunlight sneaking through the trees, or the laughter shared during a simple conversation. I’m not someone who chases loud attention; I prefer the kind of presence that speaks quietly but meaningfully. I believe every person I meet has a story, and I try to understand people beyond their first impression, giving them space to express who they really are.

Project

In the far reaches of the northern world, where the wind carved scars into the earth and the sea roared like a wounded beast, a boy once stood upon the frozen shore and wondered what it meant to live without chains. He had seen warriors raise their swords toward the heavens as if demanding answers from gods who never listened, had heard men shout of glory while the ground beneath them drank their blood, and had watched villages burn under the cold, indifferent stare of the moon. Yet even in those flames, even in that endless winter, he felt something stirring within him a tiny ember refusing to be extinguished. It was the memory of warmth from a distant past: a father's voice telling him that true strength was not in taking life, but in protecting it; a mother's trembling hands reminding him that the world was wide, and beyond its cruelty existed places where the soil was soft and nothing grew from hatred. Still, as he walked through fields of corpses and heard the dying whisper their regrets into the wind, he couldn't believe such a place truly existed. The world he knew was built upon revenge, sharpened by grief, and fed by the endless hunger of men who mistook violence for purpose. Every battle shaped him, every scream echoed in his chest, every sword swing carved deeper into his soul until he could no longer tell whether he was chasing justice or running from his own emptiness. He had once believed enemies were monsters wearing human faces, but as he stared into their terrified eyes, he realized they were simply reflections of himself lost, wounded, desperate for someone to teach them how to be more than the weapons their lives had forged them into.

Warriors marched, kings plotted, and the world continued its ceaseless cycle of conquest, but he began to understand that the strongest man was not the one who conquered nations, but the one who conquered his own heart. In the dead silence of winter nights, he dreamed of a land untouched by anger, where wheat fields swayed like golden waves and children ran freely without fear of arrows falling from the sky. A place where the scars of the past could dissolve into the earth and new seeds could grow in their place. Vinland — a word whispered like a prayer, a promise, a distant horizon shimmering beyond the reach of blood-stained hands. And though he carried the weight of countless sins, though his past clung to him like a shadow that refused to die, he kept walking. Through storms that threatened to break him, through loneliness that hollowed his chest, through battles that tested the very meaning of his existence — he walked. Because somewhere within him, beneath all the rage and sorrow, lived a vow that no sword could cut: that he would never again become a slave to hatred. That he would break the chains passed down through generations of warriors. That he would build a life worthy of the loved ones he lost. And with every step, he felt the world shift — not because the world had changed, but because he had. He learned that mercy was harder than murder, that forgiveness weighed heavier than steel, and that true courage was not born on the battlefield but in the quiet refusal to repeat the sins of the past.
